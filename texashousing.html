Dallas-Fort Worth House Prices and Trends

Visualize the change in median house prices with a map view of the DFW metroplex by zip code from 2012 - 2021.

January 15, 2022



Background:
As an aspiring homeowner in the DFW metroplex, I wanted to compare the region's housing market to national trends. According to Forbes , the median listing price of a residential home in December 2021 was $375,000. Additionally, the cost of a 2,000 sq. ft. home increased by a remarkable 18.6% from 2020.



Process:
I located a dataset encompassing the Texas market over the past ten years (2012 - 2021) courtesy of Redfin.com . These data included several housing metrics delineated by zip code.


I desired the ability to later group and filter these data by broader regions than zip code; however, city and county information was absent from the dataset. I created a table detailing the city and county for each zip code to resolve this issue. This information was later joined to the original table using SQL.


The two data tables were imported into a relational database using pgAdmin4. Additional housing metrics, such as the monthly percent change in the median sale price, were calculated with SQL during the querying process.
